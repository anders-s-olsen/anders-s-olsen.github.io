abstract: The development of appropriate models for dynamic functional connectivity
  is imperative to gain a better understanding of the brain both during rest and while
  performing a task. Leading eigenvector dynamics analysis is among the favored methods
  for assessing frame-wise connectivity, but eigenvectors are distributed on the sign-symmetric
  unit hypersphere, which is typically disregarded during modeling. Here we develop
  both mixture model and Hidden Markov model formulations for two sign-symmetric spherical
  statistical distributions and display their performance on synthetic data and task-fMRI
  data involving a finger-tapping task.
